Navigation Autopilot

Smart robots, are robots that can work in environments that were hitherto restricted to humans. Robots enter slowly the domestic part of the cleaning market, however, the professional cleaning industry, doesn't yet have the big brothers of the Roomba and the Scooba in operation.

DoBots identified two key factors that allow robots to take over the market of professional floor cleaning. First, thanks to advances in the entertainment industry, the prices for 3D depth sensors dropped from a steep price range of € 15.000,- to a stunning € 150,- (Kinect). Second, the problem of a cleaning robot is not a hardware problem, it is a software problem. Algorithms for autonomous navigation matured considerably in the last few years.

Service

There are many benefits for using robots in the floor cleaning industry.

Costs

DoBots provides it services through a service contract of € 100,- per month. The autopilot adds € 1000,- to the cleaning machine. Compare this with € 20,- per hour for a cleaning person. Saving only one cleaning hour per day, amounts to saving € 660,- per month.

Personnel diversification

DoBots wants to create services that work for and with people. Diversification of personnel: allows the creation of professional ladders (certificates), more diverse offerings to customers, and enriches interactions between colleagues. Cleaning activities should be fun: robots get the boring parts, controlling a robot empowers.

Always the best

Currently there is a mis-alignment between the user and the manufacturer of a cleaning machine. The user wants a clean floor, but pays for a machine. By paying a service fee, the cleaning company becomes responsible for the machine, takes care of it, and upgrades it continuously with the newest brushes, batteries, and sweeping strategies.

Functionality

The autopilot has navigation, localization, and sweeping functionality.

Navigation

A map can be uploaded to the robot, or the robot can autonomously chart an area. Changes in the environment, such as containers, products, or people are incorporated automatically.

Localization

A robot always knows where it is. There will be no lost equipment. An anti-theft alarm system is included. In manual operation or with remote operation, the localization of the robot can be tracked as well.

Sweeping

The scrubber/dryer sweeps according to a self-created plan given the area to maintain. This can also be manually overridden by the end user in a friendly graphical interface.

Status

Status of the device and the environment are easily monitored.

Battery

The autopilot knows the state of the batteries. Thanks to its localization capabilities it can find a recharge station. Deep discharging will be prevented automatically by communication to the maintenance team. The lifetime of the robot is greatly enhanced by monitoring for required maintenance.

Coverage

The robot knows where it went. The state of the floor can be directly reported, and truthfully so. Different from a human, a robot can operate in a 24-hour setting, in "performance-based" settings.

Water and soap

The level of water and soap are easily displayed to a cleaning person. In robotizing the mechanics of the cleaning machine it is also possible to refill automatically.
